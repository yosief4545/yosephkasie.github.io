Professional Self-Assessment

Completing the coursework in the Computer Science program and developing this ePortfolio has helped me better understand my strengths, shape my professional goals,and prepare for a career in computer science. Throughout the program,I have built skills in software design, algorithms and data structures, database development, and security. At the beginning of the program, I was mainly focused on learning programming and completing assignments,but over time I learned how different areas of computer science connect to solve real problems. Building this ePortfolio gave me the opportunity to reflect on that growth and present clear evidence of my technical abilities.It also helped me see how my projects support my long-term career interests in software development and cybersecurity.

One important area of development throughout the program has been collaboration and communication. Through discussions, project narratives, design documents, and milestone submissions, I practiced explaining technical topics clearly and professionally. This strengthened my ability to communicate with stakeholders by thinking about what different audiences need to understand. Some assignments required me to explain technical systems in simpler terms for nontechnical users, while others required more technical depth and precision. This ability to adapt communication is important in the workplace because computer scientists often work with managers, clients, team members, and users with different levels of technical knowledge.

My work with data structures and algorithms helped me develop stronger problem-solving skills and a better understanding of efficiency, trade-offs, and design decisions. In my algorithms and data structures artifact, I used a binary search tree to organize and search course information in a course planner. Enhancing that artifact helped me strengthen the reliability, readability, and maintainability of the program while preserving its original purpose.

My software design and engineering experience helped me understand how to build and improve applications in a more organized, maintainable, and user-centered way. Across the program, I worked on projects that required planning, coding, testing, revising, and documenting software solutions. In my software design and engineering artifact, I focused on improving the quality and structure of an existing application so that it better reflected professional standards.

My database coursework and artifact development helped me build skills in data management, backend programming, and secure handling of system resources. In my database artifact, I used a Python CRUD module connected to MongoDB to manage records in the Grazioso Salvare project. Enhancing this artifact helped me focus on stronger validation, better error handling, improved reliability, and more professional database code.

Security has also become an important part of the way I think about computer science. As I progressed through the program, I began to see that security is not a separate topic added at the end of development, but a mindset that should be present throughout design, implementation, and maintenance. This perspective is especially meaningful to me because I am interested in cybersecurity as a career path.

The artifacts in this ePortfolio fit together to present a broad view of my growth in the Computer Science program. The code review introduces my ability to analyze existing work, identify issues, and plan meaningful improvements. The software design and engineering enhancement demonstrates my understanding of improving software structure and quality. The algorithms and data structures enhancement highlights my problem-solving ability and my use of efficient computing solutions. The database enhancement demonstrates my knowledge of backend data management and database reliability. Together, these artifacts show that I can evaluate systems, improve them thoughtfully, and explain my technical work clearly.
